The Jim line might be her experience with a boyfriend or maybe a friends experience with someone who was abusive. It also seems she is reflecting on this toxic relationship and it seems like she is longing for the past which doesnt make sense to me since it seems like she was emotionally and physically manipulated.

I wonder if ultraviolence means something else but this is what I interpret it as her love being extremely intense and painful.

The sirens i think has two meanings/metaphors which could be sirens from an ambulance or police car. The ambulance meaning could be that she is hurt and needs help or the police car meaning could be that she is in trouble and needs to be rescued. The second one could be sirens from greek mythology which they are know for luring sailors to their death with their voices. This could mean that she is being lured into a toxic relationship and she knows it (at least I hope she knows) but she still wants to be in it. Which is kind of messed up and its pissing me off.

The violins could represent the beauty and love of the relationship. The violins and the sirens might make it seem like she is caught in this cycle of love and violence and can't escape it.

The "give me all of that ultraviolence" line could mean that she is addicted to the pain and the love and can't get enough of it. Which again she goes on about a very toxic relationship and I hope its just a song and not a actual experience she or someone she knows went through.

In the poison ivy line it seems to imply that her boyfriend is manipulating her into thinking that she is the problem in the relationship and/or she is "poisoning" everyone around her. (What an emotional manipulative bitch.)

In the next line it seems like she is saying that he was always there for her, but in a way that was harmful. The line "I could've died right then" suggests that she felt a deep emotional pain, but he was there to support her through it, which is a twisted form of love.

The next line makes it seem like Jim gave her a sense of purpose kind of like he built her up emotionally probably from a time when she was feeling lost and alone. This probably implies that she was dependent on him for her self-worth and identity. Which is why everytime he hurt her it felt like love because that is what she associates love with.

Lana seemed to realize that not matter how much she loved Jim, it would never be enough to make the relationship work. That messed with her head and made her question her own worth.
